# Compiled Virus Research Vector Database

## Influenza A
- Influenza A begins with respiratory inoculation and early replication in nasal, throat, and tracheal epithelium.
- Common early symptoms include fever, malaise, sore throat, dry cough, chills, and myalgia.
- Lower respiratory spread can involve the lungs and produce dyspnea or viral pneumonia in severe cases.
- Immune activation and systemic inflammation contribute to fatigue and persistent constitutional symptoms.
- Recovery follows clearance of infected epithelial cells, although cough and fatigue may linger.

## SARS-CoV-2 (COVID-19)
- SARS-CoV-2 initially infects the upper airway and can progress to lower respiratory tract involvement.
- Cough, fever, fatigue, sore throat, and dyspnea are common manifestations.
- Severe disease can involve diffuse lung inflammation, hypoxemia, and systemic immune dysregulation.

## Respiratory Syncytial Virus (RSV)
- RSV commonly affects the upper airway first, with progression to bronchioles and lungs in more severe cases.
- Cough, wheeze, congestion, and shortness of breath are frequent clinical features.

## Hepatitis B Virus
- Hepatitis B targets hepatocytes and may produce fatigue, jaundice, nausea, and elevated liver inflammation.
- Disease progression reflects viral replication in the liver and host immune-mediated hepatocellular injury.
